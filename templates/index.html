About MangoCare

MangoCare is an innovative AI-powered platform dedicated to assisting mango farmers in identifying and managing plant diseases with precision and efficiency. Our mission is to enhance mango cultivation by providing accurate, timely, and actionable insights, enabling farmers to protect their crops, reduce losses, and increase productivity.

Leveraging state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms and a comprehensive database of mango diseases, MangoCare analyzes images of mango leaves to detect a wide range of common and critical diseases. By offering early detection, our platform empowers farmers to take proactive measures, ensuring the health and vitality of their mango orchards.

Key Features:

  • Accurate Detection : Utilizes advanced AI models to ensure precise identification of mango plant diseases.
  • Comprehensive Database : Covers seven common mango diseases, including Anthracnose, Bacterial Canker, Cutting Weevil, Die Back, Gall Midge, Powdery Mildew, and Sooty Mould.
  • User-Friendly Interface : Designed for ease of use, allowing farmers of all technical backgrounds to navigate and utilize the platform effectively.
  • Insights : Provides detailed descriptions and recommended treatments for each detected disease, aiding in informed decision-making.
  • Mobile Accessibility : Accessible on various devices, enabling farmers to use the tool in the field with ease.

At MangoCare, we are committed to supporting the agricultural community by integrating technology with traditional farming practices. Our platform not only helps in disease detection but also educates farmers on best practices for disease management and prevention, fostering sustainable and profitable mango farming.

Common Mango Diseases

Anthracnose

Black lesions caused by fungal infections.

Bacterial Canker

Lesions and dieback caused by bacteria.

Powdery Mildew

White, powdery fungal growth on leaves.

Cutting Weevil

A pest that damages mango stems and fruits.

Die Back

A fungal disease causing branches to wither and die.

Gall Midge

Insects that form galls on leaves, reducing yield.

Sooty Mould

Black, sooty patches caused by fungal infections.

Healthy

No visible disease detected.
